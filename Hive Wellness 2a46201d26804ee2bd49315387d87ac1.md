# Hive Wellness

## **Defining the Problem:**

Chronic Stress is a large and pervasive problem impacting employees’ health and productivity and is costing employers billions of dollars every year. There is a need for a more whole-person approach to managing chronic stress. 

## Design Process - Phase 1:

We first started by understanding the client and aligning with their business goals and the best way to achieve that is - **To use lightning talks to draft our HMW’s**

The lightning talks by our client gave us insight and a head start to draft our How Might We's. Our first HMW is 

## *“How Might We enable patients to have a platform that encourages them to be responsible for their own health where everything is under one roof”‍*

### Aligning with Business Goals

The business concerns were to Focus on user experience that promotes high value, simplicity and makes the user stick.- Experiment and validate with the market before build.- Address budgetary concerns around development, marketing, etc.

## Phase 2:

In the second part, we have outlined the user goal after analyzing the research points along with a customer persona for a better understanding of the user.‍

- **What does the user want to achieve?** - To be able to understand the cause of chronic disease and get relief and healthy for a longer-term rather than just taking allopathic treatment.
- **Who are we solving for?** - Using the information provided by our client we created a persona for whom we would be solving the problem.

## Phase 4:

Using user stories to think like a user.

‍
I and my colleague along with the product manager framed bite-sized user stories. This helped to build context in terms of the job the user is trying to get done in each stage.

- As a user, I want to book an appointment for a consultation
- As a user, I want to connect with someone who can guide me about my health issues
- As a user, I want to track my own wellness
- As a user, I want to monitor my wellness plan and courses

# Outcome

The show-and-tell prototype and designs helped the client take a proposal to potential investors and tell a story about how their solution might reduce time to market. This is yet to be pushed to production.

<aside>
✨ **What did I learn? :)**

The domain was interesting to learn and it helped me understand a different aspect of healthcare and its gaps and how one can design for keeping the end patient in the mind.

</aside>

### Takeaway 1 - How to deal with ambiguity

This was an MVP idea and being true to its nature, there was a lot of ambiguity involved so I had to make sure that I wasn’t deviating from the client's needs
‍

### Takeaway 2 - Change is inevitable

While working on this project, the team had to deal with constant changes and at times it was difficult to converge to a solution. I learned to cater to sticking to one solution for the time being and use feedback as a way to improve the design*.*
