HealthTech Mobile App Proof of Concept UX Design Design Sprint

HIVE
WELLNESS

Designing a proof of concept prototype for a healthcare platform that empowers users to take charge of their own health — providing everything needed under one roof.

My Role
Interaction / Visual Design · User Research · Sketching · Prototyping
Team
Me 🙂 · Akanksha Biswas (UXD) · Naveen Chevuri (PM)
Duration
6 Weeks — Design Sprint & Proof of Concept Testing
The Context

Ever found yourself lost in the sea of information?

After a quick Google search on a health topic, do you save articles for later-reading, or find yourself confused by the multitude of suggestions — unsure of the next steps or who to consult online?

What if there's a solution aimed at empowering you to manage your health by providing everything required in one platform?

Hive Wellness is envisioned by 2 individuals who wanted to create a platform that could empower users to take charge of their own health — providing everything needed under one roof.

🏆 Honorable mention: Two of the startups I worked with partnered with each other — bringing this vision to life as a proof-of-concept that went on to attract potential investors.

Before Doing Anything

Clarifying the Research Goals

To understand the problem deeply, we structured our research around four pillars: Value Proposition, Product, User Goals, and Obstacles — asking stakeholders pointed questions to uncover business objectives, product purpose, and what a user-friendly solution would look like.

Lightning Talks

Getting into the Stakeholders' Minds

We conducted lightning talk sessions with the founders to understand their perspective on the product's scope, the core problem, their vision, and business goals. Three key themes emerged:

🧩
Problems Identified

Lack of Clarity — users carry out research but struggle to act. Lack of health literacy — people believe technology should manage health without user involvement. Lack of ownership — people rely on healthcare professionals rather than taking personal responsibility.

💼
Business Goals

User retention — provide everything needed to keep users on the platform. Market validation — achieve product-market fit by holding for users and testing with them. Cost of development — achieve proof of concept to share with investors.

🚀
Opportunities

Educate the user — through small courses, individuals can learn more steps to develop their health holistically. Track wellness — including sleep, fitness, activities. Connect to expert — patients often find it difficult to understand their issue even after speaking with a consultant.

"HMW enable patients to have a platform that empowers them to be responsible for their own health management — all under one roof?"

The term "Everything" felt ambiguous. We wanted to have more specifics about what users actually expect from a platform that encompasses everything. Unfortunately, we didn't have the opportunity to interview actual target users — so we started by torturing the stakeholders with bad designs (examples bruh!).

Stakeholder Pain Mapping

Current Experience Gaps

We asked stakeholders to share their negative experiences, which apps/websites they avoid and why — identifying fears and myths to map the current experience gaps:

📋
Multiple Questionnaires

Users face multiple questionnaires and receive numerous recommendations on which doctor to consult — creating decision paralysis. (Hick's Law violation)

🔖
Always Saving for Later

Redirecting to suggested articles or courses can be helpful, but users frequently save them for later rather than actually completing them. (Zeigarnik Effect opportunity)

📈
Users Like to Track Progress

Users often desire to track their progress, whether for articles or courses — but existing platforms rarely make this visible or rewarding. (Goal Gradient Effect)

🤖
Lack of Delightfulness

Many healthcare experiences lack delight, feeling more robotic than engaging — killing motivation for consistent use. (Aesthetic-Usability Effect)

Our primary research revealed that Hive's target audience comprises individuals who visit hospitals regularly, often due to a lack of proper guidance and direction. Trey effectively represents these primary user groups.

Utilising the Jobs to Be Done Framework

Making a Smoother Ride Instead of Reinventing the Wheel 😄

We listed all the tasks that Trey might need to accomplish to get his job done — mapping them into a comprehensive user flow. It might look simple, but involves many interdependent tasks. Our goal was to ensure everyone was informed about and aligned on the proposed solution.

We translated all tasks into a user flow, which allowed the team to begin sketching and designing the main screens. Each team member came up with 2–4 sketches individually, then we regrouped to find common themes and align on direction and style.

Sketches Before Stretches

Ambiguity = Iterations

We gathered around the drawing board and quickly sketched ideas. Each of us came up with 2–4 sketches individually, then regrouped to find common themes — and talk about the direction and style envisioned for the project. Ambiguity is the key part of this project — and I learned to deal with it.

Our Final Solution

Five Core Features That Define Hive

🏠
Landing Screen
🤖

📊
Wellness Plan
📅
Book Appointment
  • 01
    Chatbot assistant that guides users towards a wellness plan — The flow is designed to identify user needs and create an actionable wellness plan. Upon completing the survey, this plan enables users to access the right care at the right time.
  • 02
    Nudging users to book appointments (mental health check) — This process helps users understand their mental health and nudges them towards booking expert appointments. It fulfils user needs while also serving the business objectives of the product — connecting with health experts and generating revenue.
  • 03
    Conduct daily check-ins to empower users — Users log their feelings and earn wellness points daily. The purpose is to foster emotional connectivity and empower users to take care of their health — much like Snap streaks.
  • 04
    Complete recommended courses and articles — Personalised wellness courses with progress tracking. My Wellness Points gamify engagement and motivate course completion. (Goal Gradient Effect)
  • 05
    Track wellness through smart devices — Help users receive updates about appointments, fitness and sleep reports by linking smart devices with an easy-to-set-up interface handling smartwatches.
Our Initial Iterations

Some Iterations & Why They Didn't Work

We stitched together the flow and conducted concept testing with users through Google Meet sessions, identifying the following issues:

✗ Before — Landing Screen
"Better health starts here."

Simple title but unclear instructions — How can a user understand and interpret what Hive Wellness is offering? Not so clear entry point — Users could get started, but it may potentially lead to frustration later.

✓ After — Improved
"All things about your wellness"

Clear instructions and actions — Users can immediately understand what Hive Wellness offers and choose categories based on their requirements. (Jakob's Law · Clarity principle)

✗ Before — Chatbot
Tips & nudges during questions

These nudges appeared good on screen, but during testing they served more as a distraction than a benefit — increasing cognitive load on users and sidetracking them from answering questions. (Miller's Law violation)

✓ After — Simplified
Removed nudges, improved focus

Removed the nudges and focused solely on the questions — improving user focus and reducing cognitive load during the assessment flow. (Hick's Law · Cognitive Load Theory)

Here Comes My Superpower 🧑‍🎨

Micro-interactions for Better Engagement

I used simple, friendly and healthy 😉 illustrations and prototyped micro-interactions to infuse vibrancy into the platform and create a positive atmosphere. Each interaction was designed to reduce anxiety around health topics and make the experience feel less clinical.

  • 🎉
    Trey, I am glad you are happy today! — Celebration moments after check-ins reinforce positive emotional connection with the app.
  • ✅
    Appointment confirmed — Clear, reassuring confirmation screens with calendar integration (Add to iCal) reduce anxiety and build trust.
  • 🔍
    Analysis loading state — "Based on your responses, I am finding a suitable plan for you" — a human-feeling transition that sets expectations and maintains engagement during processing.

Reflections

Ambiguity is the key part of this project — and I learned to deal with it.

This is an MVP, and being true to its nature, there was a lot of ambiguity involved. I had to make sure I was not deviating from the client's needs while still advocating for the user — a balancing act that sharpened my design decision-making.

Managing change and converging at a point.

The team had to deal with constant changes, and at times it became hard to converge. I made sure to prioritise the tasks and path clearly — articulating direction to the team and keeping us aligned despite the ambiguity.
